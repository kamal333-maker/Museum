About The Bard

William Shakespeare (1564–1616) was an English playwright, poet, and actor. His plays — tragedies, comedies, and histories — reshaped English drama and introduced countless words and phrases to the English language.

Early Life

Born in Stratford-upon-Avon on April 23, 1564. Educated at the local grammar school, he quickly showed a flair for language and performance.

Major Works

Some signature works: Hamlet, Macbeth, Othello, King Lear, and Romeo and Juliet.

Legacy

Credited with coining or popularizing hundreds of words and phrases (over 1,700 word usages are often attributed to him).
